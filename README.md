# LIVER-DISEASE-PREDICTION
The liver is the largest solid organ in the human body and performs more than 500 vital functions, including metabolism, protein synthesis, detoxification, bile production, and immune regulation. As a result, liver disease can have serious effects on overall health. Liver diseases include viral hepatitis, alcoholic liver disease, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH), autoimmune conditions, inherited disorders, cirrhosis, and liver cancer. These conditions often develop gradually over many years and may remain undetected in their early stages.

Liver disease is a major global health challenge, causing over two million deaths annually. Rising rates of obesity, diabetes, alcohol consumption, and chronic viral infections have contributed to an increasing burden worldwide. In the United Kingdom, liver-related mortality continues to rise, unlike many other major diseases. South Asian populations are particularly vulnerable to NAFLD because of genetic and lifestyle factors.

One of the greatest challenges in managing liver disease is its silent progression. Early-stage patients may experience only mild symptoms, such as fatigue, or no symptoms at all. Severe signs, including jaundice and cirrhosis, often appear only when the disease is advanced, limiting treatment options. Traditional diagnostic methods include liver function tests, imaging techniques, and liver biopsy. Although effective, these approaches have limitations related to cost, accessibility, invasiveness, and the need for expert interpretation.

Machine learning has emerged as a promising tool to support liver disease diagnosis. By analysing multiple clinical features simultaneously, machine learning models can identify patterns and predict disease risk more consistently than traditional methods alone. These systems are particularly valuable for screening and early detection in primary care and resource-limited settings. Rather than replacing clinicians, they aim to assist decision-making and improve patient outcomes. This research therefore investigates supervised machine learning techniques using the Indian Liver Patient Dataset to classify liver disease accurately while considering transparency, evaluation metrics, and ethical issues in healthcare applications.
